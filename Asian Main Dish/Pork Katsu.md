# Pork Katsu

## 1. Shopping List
- [ ] **2** boneless pork loins or chops
- [ ] **1** egg
- [ ] **1 cup** panko bread crumbs
- [ ] **½ cup** all-purpose flour
- [ ] Table salt
- [ ] Black pepper powder
- [ ] **1-2L** of canola oil

## 2. Preparation
|<!-- -->|<!-- -->|
|---|---|
| **2** pork loins | **1/2 cup** of all-purpose flour |
| **1 tsp** of salt | **1 tsp** of black pepper powder |
| **1** egg | **1 cup** of panko bread crumbs |

1. Use a small frypan to evenly pound the chops to approximately **¾ inch** thickness.
2. Reserve **1 tbsp** of flour. Add the rest, along with salt and black pepper, to a bowl and mix.
3. Dust each pork chop thoroughly shake off the excess.
4. Add **1 tbsp** of flour to the eggs and beat to create an egg wash.
5. Dip each floured pork chop into the egg mixture, allowing excess to drip off.
6. Transfer to the pork chops into a tray of panko bread crumbs and press firmly on both sides

## 3. Double Fry
|<!-- -->|<!-- -->|
|---|---|
| **1-2L** of canola oil | |

1. Heat oil to **320°F** over medium heat. This lower temperature is suitable for the initial fry.
2. Gently lower the pork cutlet into the oil and fry for **3 minutes** per side, until the oil isn't bubbling as aggressively.
3. Remove with a skimmer and place on a paper towel-lined plate or wire rack to drain. 
4. Repeat with the second cutlet.
5. Increase oil temperature to **350°F** for the second fry.
6. Gently lower each cutlet back into the hot oil and fry for **1 minute** per side, until the panko coating is deep golden brown and crispy.
7. Remove cutlets and place on a clean paper towel-lined plate or wire rack to drain.
