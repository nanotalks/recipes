# Raspberry Blondies

## 1. Shopping List
- [ ] 4 large eggs
- [ ] 400g fresh raspberries
- [ ] 1 lemon
- [ ] 2 cups of all-purpose flour
- [ ] 1.25 cups granulated sugar
- [ ] 3/4 cup of unsalted butter
- [ ] Artificial vanilla extract
- [ ] Baking powder
- [ ] Table salt
- [ ] Cornstarch

## 2. Raspberry Puree
|<!-- -->|<!-- -->|
|---|---|
| **400g** of fresh raspberries | **20g** of sugar |
| **1 tbsp** of lemon juice | **1.5 tbsp** of cornstarch |

1. Blend raspberries into puree.
2. Strain to remove all seeds.
3. Add puree, sugar and lemon juice into a pot. Stir to combine.
4. Place on medium heat and bring to a boil.
5. Mix cornstarch with **1.5 tbsp** of water and add to pot. 
6. Stir until the mixture thickens, about 2–3 minutes.
5. Remove from heat and let cool in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes.

## 3. Batter & Bake
|<!-- -->|<!-- -->|
|---|---|
| **200g** of granulated sugar | **180g** of unsalted butter, melted |
| **4** large eggs | **1 tbsp** of vanilla extract |
| **300g** of all-purpose flour | **1 tsp** of baking powder |
| **1/2 tsp** of salt | |

1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
2. Cut out and line a baking tray with parchment paper.
3. Whip sugar, eggs, and vanilla extract together, until the mixture lightens in color and increases in volume, about 3 min.
4. Add in the butter and whisk until combined.
5. Whisk in the all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt until just combined.
6. Add half of the batter into the baking pan and smooth out.
7. Add dollops of cooled raspberry puree over this and swirl around.
8. Add the remaining batter, followed by the remaining puree. Swirl again.
9. Bake for around 25 min.
10. Test with a toothpick, inserted into the center of the cake. Continue baking and testing in 10min increments until the toothpick comes out with clean of batter.
11. Let cake cool on a wire rack for 20mins, then  chill in the fridge for another 1 hour before serving
