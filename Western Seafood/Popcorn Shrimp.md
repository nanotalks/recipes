# Popcorn Shrimp

## 1. Shopping List
- [ ] **1 lb** shrimp
- [ ] **1** egg
- [ ] **1 cup** all-purpose flour
- [ ] **1/4 cup** cornstarch
- [ ] **1/4 cup** milk
- [ ] **1-2L** canola oil
- [ ] [Asian-Cajun Spice][1]
- [ ] [Tartar Sauce][2]

## 2. Preparation and Coating
|<!-- -->|<!-- -->|
|---|---|
| **1 lb** of large shrimp, peeled and deveined | **1 cup** of all-purpose flour |
| **1/4 cup** of cornstarch | **2 tbsp** of Asian-Cajun seasoning |
| **1** egg | **1/4 cup** of milk |

1. Pat the shrimp dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture.
2. Toss the shrimps in **1/4 cup** of flour, until all the shrimps are evenly coated
3. In a shallow bowl, combine remaining all-purpose flour, cornstarch and Asian-Cajun seasoning
4. In another shallow bowl, beat the egg and milk together.
5. Working in batches of 8-10 pieces, dip the shrimp into the beaten egg, and coat in the seasoned dry ingredients, pressing gently so the coating adheres.

## 3. Frying
|<!-- -->|<!-- -->|
|---|---|
| **1-2L** canola oil | **2 tbsp** of tartar sauce |

1. Heat oil to 375°F in a pot.
2. Gently lower the coated shrimp into the oil in batches of 8-10 pieces.
3. Fry for 2 minutes, until the coating is golden brown and crispy.
4. Remove the shrimp and place on a paper towel-lined plate or wire-rack.
5. Repeat with remaining shrimp batches.
6. Serve with tartar sauce as dipping sauces.
